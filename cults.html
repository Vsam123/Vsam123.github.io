What is a Cult

Cults refer to groups or movements that exhibit extreme devotion to a specific person, idea, or belief. Such groups usually have a charismatic leader who exerts significant influence over their members and utilizes manipulative or coercive tactics to maintain control. Often, cults promote beliefs or practices that are considered controversial or outside of mainstream society. In some cases, cults may also engage in illegal activities or pose a threat to their members or society as a whole.

Types of cults and their practices:

Scientology:

Scientology is a religion that was founded in the 1950s by science fiction writer L. Ron Hubbard. It is based on the idea that humans are immortal beings known as "Thetans" who have lived many past lives and that our current problems stem from negative experiences in those past lives. Back to the point, Scientology teaches that individuals can overcome these negative experiences through a process known as "auditing," which involves a form of counseling that is meant to help individuals become more aware of their past experiences and emotions.

Scientology has been controversial due to its secretive nature and its alleged use of manipulative and coercive tactics on its members. It has also faced legal challenges, including allegations of fraud and abuse. However, the organization maintains that it is a legitimate religion and has a significant following around the world.
